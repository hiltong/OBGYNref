Preterm Labor

  • With preterm labor obtain GBS culture and start GBS antibiotic prophylaxis (if GBS culture within 5 preceding weeks, previous GBS infected newborn or GBS bacteriuria in current pregnancy use to guide management).1
    • Continue GBS antibiotic prophylaxis until delivery if preterm labor progresses.1
    • Discontinue GBS antibiotic prophylaxis if preterm labor does not progress:1
      • GBS culture not available with recurrent preterm labor:1
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis.1
        • Obtain GBS culture if not done previously.1
      • GBS culture positive with recurrent preterm or term labor:1
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis.1
        • GBS culture not repeated (like previous GBS infected newborn or GBS bacteriuria).1
      • GBS culture negative in 5 week window with recurrent preterm labor:1
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis "not necessary".1
      • GBS culture negative out of 5 week window with recurrent preterm labor:1
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis.1
        • Repeat GBS culture.1
      • GBS culture negative out of 5 week window and no labor:1
        • Repeat GBS culture at 36 0/7 - 37 6/7 weeks.1

5 week "screening accuracy window" based on early term screening studies and preterm data lacking1

  • Indications
    • Preterm labor < 32 weeks.2
  • Contraindications
    • Platlet dysfunction or bleeding disorder.2
    • Asthma with aspirin hypersensitivty.2
    • GI ulcerative disease.2
    • Hepatic dysfunction.2
    • Renal dysfunction.2
  • Risks (especially > 48 hours use)
    • Constriction of ductus arteriosus.2
    • Oligohydraminos.2
    • Necrotizing enterocolitis in preterm neonates.2
    • Possibly patent ductus arteriosus in neonates.2
  • Dose
    • Loading dose 50 mg orally.1
    • 25 mg orally every 6 hours for 7 doses (48 hours total).1

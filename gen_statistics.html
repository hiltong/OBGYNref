  • Indicates how likely a patient has a disease. Ratios range from zero to infinity. The higher the ratio the more likely the disease. The lower the ratio means the less likley the disease.3
  • This ratio indicates how many times more likely a positive result is to happen in a patient with the condition than it would in a patient without the condition.3
  • True positive/False positive
    • 0 to 1: Decrease likelihood of disease. The closer to zero the higher decrease in likelihood of disease.3
    • 1: No diagnostic value.3
    • Above 1: Increase likelihood of disease. The farther away from 1 the higher the likelihood of disease. Ratios over 10 very high likelihood of disease.3
  • The sensivity of a test tells me if I have a disease what percentage of the time a test will be positive. If I don't know my disease status the positive predictive value of a test tells me how likley I am to have the disease if the test is positive.1
  • The specificity of a test tells me if I don't have a disease what percentage of the time a test will be negative. If I don't know my disease status the negative predictive value of a test tells me how likley I am to not have the disease if the test is negative.1

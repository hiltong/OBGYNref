Operative vaginal birth

  • Possible fetal compromise.1
  • Prolonged second stage of labor.1
  • Shorten second stage of labor for maternal indications (eg, cardiac).
  • Unengaged fetal head.1
  • Unknown position of fetal head.1
  • Possible fetal bone demineralization disorder:1
    • Osteogenesis imperfecta.1
  • Possible fetal bleeding disorder:1
    • Alloimmune thrombocytopenia.1
    • Hemophilia.1
    • von Willebrand disease.1
  • < 34 weeks vacuum extraction discouraged.1
  • Sequential vacuum extractor/forceps discouraged secondary to increased neonatal complications.1
  • Outlet forceps:1
    • Fetal scalp is visible at the introitus without separating the labia.1
    • Fetal skull has reached the pelvic floor.1
    • Fetal head is at or on perineum.1
    • OA, ROA or LOA.1
    • Rotation ≤ 45 degrees.1
  • Low forceps:1
    • Station ≥ +2 cm and not on the pelvic floor.1
    • Without rotation: Rotation ≤ 45 degrees.1
    • With rotation: Rotation > 45 degrees.1
  • Midforceps:1
    • Station > +2 cm but head engaged.1
  • Forceps more likely to result in vaginal birth.1
  • Foreceps more likley to have 3rd or 4th degree tear.1

Ovulation Prediction

  • Menstrual chart
  • BBT
  • Serum progesterone in "suspected" midluteal phase
  • Urinary ovulation predictor kits
  • Related topics
  • References
  • Regular 25 - 35 day cycles, especially if molimina symptoms, usually ovulatory.2
  • Basal temperature increases 0.3-0.5 degrees after ovulation.1
    • Secondary to progesterone effect on hypothalamus to increase body temperature.2
  • Temperature rise begins 1-2 days after the LH surge and lasts at least 10 days. Fall in temperature indicator of near onset of menses.2
  • Retrospectively identifies ovulation but occurs too late to time intercourse or procedures.2
  • Mid-luteal phase = 18 - 24 days after onset of menses or 7 days before next menses expected.2
  • Normal mid-luteal phase progesterone levels 6 - 25 ng/ml.2
    • Presumed ovulatory if > 3 ng/ml.1
    • Variability in single progesterone levels as progesterone levels increase with LH pulsations that occur after ovulation.2
      • Single low progesterone may not be abnormal as could be between LH pulses, consider repeating in 2-3 days.2
  • Physiology:
    • LH surge occurs ~ 36 hours before ovulation and lasts ~ 48 hours.2
      • Serum LH surge detected ~ 36 hours before ovulation.2
      • Urine LH surge detected ~ 24 hours before ovulation (i.e., ~ 12 hours after serum LH surge detected).2

      • Oocyte can be fertilized up to 24 hours after ovulation.3

      Sperm most capable of fertilization up to 48 hours after enter female genital tract.3

  • Kits detect urinary LH 24-36 hours before ovulation, what patient sees:1
    • Negative test.1
    • Positive test for 1-2 days during ovulatory period.1
    • Negative test when LH returns to baseline.1
    • If does not conceive menses occurs 12-16 days after ovulation.1
  • False positives with urinary ovulation predictor kits:
    • PCOS can have tonically elevated LH concentrations resulting in positive LH kits with anovulatory bleeding (i.e., kits positive too many days long and positive too close to bleeding).1
    • Primary ovarian insufficiency & menopause.2
    • Fertility medications.1
    • Pregnancy (LH similar to hCG).1

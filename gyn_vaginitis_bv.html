Bacterial Vaginosis

  • Prepuberty and postmenopausal women not on estrogen.1
  • Women who have never been sexually active.1
  • Recommended: 1
    • Amsel Criteria (Need 3 of 4):1
      1. Discharge: homogeneous, thin, white-gray discharge.1
      2. Vaginal pH: > 4.5.1
      3. KOH whiff test: positive.1
      4. Clue cells: > 20% (since only need 3 criteria can diagnose without microscopy).1
    • Gram Stain With Nugent Scoring:1
      • Reference standard but impractical.1
  • Alternative: 1
    • FDA approved commercial tests:1
      • Can also detect trichomoniasis and candidiasis.1
  • Recommended options:1
    • Metronidazole 500 mg orally BID x 7 days.1
      • No alcohol during and for 24 hours after treatment.1
    • Metronidazole 0.75% gel 1 applicator (5 g) vaginally bedtime x 5 days.1
    • Clindamycin 2% cream 1 applicator (5 g) vaginally bedtime x 7 days.1
  • Alternate options:1
    • Secnidazole 2 g orally x 11
      • 1 packet (2 grams) of granules PO without regard to timing of meals .2
      • Sprinkle entire contents of the packet of secnidazole onto applesauce, yogurt, or pudding and take all the mixture within 30 minutes without chewing or crunching the granules. Drink a glass of water to aid in swallowing the mixture; secnidazole mixture is not intended to be dissolved in any liquid. Advise the patient that secnidazole may be taken without regard to the timing of meals 2
    • Tinidazole 2 g orally daily x 2 days.1
      • No alcohol during and for 72 hours after treatment.1
    • Tinidazole 1 g orally daily x 5 days.1
      • No alcohol during and for 72 hours after treatment.1
    • Clindamycin 300 mg orally BID x 7days.1
    • Clindamycin 100 mg ovules vaginally bedtime x 3 days.1
      • Base may weaken condoms and diaphragms so avoid use during and 72 hours after treatment.1

Avoid sexual activity or use condoms during treatemnt.1

  • Recurs in 30% in 3 months and 58% in 12 months.1
  • Criteria "recurrent bacterial vagonsis": 3 documented episodes in 1 year.1
  • Treatment options:1
    • Treat episode and then metronidazole gel vaginally twice weekly x 16 weeks.1
    • Change treatment or extend duration of treatment.1
  • 3.5 - 4.5:
    • Reproductive years secondary to estrogen stimulating glycogen in vaginal cells that casues colonization with lactobacillus whcih produces lactic acid.1
    • Vulvovaginal candidiasis.1
  • > 4.5:
    • Prepuberty and postmenopausal (not using estrogen).1
    • Bacterial vaginosis.1
    • Trichomoniasis.1

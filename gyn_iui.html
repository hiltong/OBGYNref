  1. Ovulation during IUI cycle.1
  2. At least one functional fallopian tube.1
  3. Adequate number of motile sperm.1
  4. No genital tract infection.1
  • Stimulated cycles:
    • Unexplained infertility. 1
    • Mild male factor infertility.1
    • Minimal or mild endometriosis (Stage I/II) and < 35.1
    • Cervical factor infertility.1
  • Unstimulated cycles:
    • Absence of fertility issues (eg, same-sex couples).1
    • Discordant for sexually transmitted disease.1
    • Sexual dysfunction (eg, severe vaginismus, ejaculatory dysfunction, retrograde ejaculation).1

Usually used prior to proceeding to IVF with these indciations.1

  • Colllection:
    • By masturbation with no lubricants in the morning after 2-3 days of abstinence.1
  • Sample adequacy:
    • Post-wash total motile sperm count = ejaculate volume x sperm conc x motility.2
    • Controversial minimum post-wash total motile sperm count for IUI:
      • > 5 million:
        • Associated with higher pregnancy rates.1
        • Superovulation with IUI pregnancy rate per cycle ~5-8%.2
      • < 5 million:
        • Superovulation with IUI pregnancy rate per cycle < 1%.2
        • Consider urological evaluation and/or IVF/ICSI.1
  • Timing IUI based on:
    • LH surge detected in serum ~ 36 hours before ovulation and in urine ~ 24 hours before ovulation.1
    • Oocyte can be fertilized up to 24 hours after ovulation.1
    • Sperm best at fertilization up to 48 hours. 1
  • Unstimulated cycle timing:
    • OTC urine ovulation predictor kit.1
    • After LH surge found in first morning specimen perform IUI next day.1
      • Pregnancy rates reported higher with urinary LH surge monitoring verses hCG trigger to time IUI in natural cycles.1
    • Stimulated cycle timing:
    • OTC urine ovulation predictor kit.1
    • After LH surge found in first morning specimen perform IUI next day.1
      • Pregnancy rates reported higher with urinary LH surge monitoring verses hCG trigger to time IUI with clomiphene citrate.1
      • Ultrasound Follicle monitoring and hCG triggering increases visits and cost.1
      • If hCG trigger used urine and serum pregnancy test may be positive up to 12 days after hCG trigger.1
  • Pregnancy rates lower after the third IUI cycle.1
  • Clomiphene citrate:
    • Standard first-line treatment.1
    • Clomiphene citrate 100 mg x 5 days beginning on cycle day 3-5.1
  • Letrozole:
    • Letrozole 5 mg x 5 days beginning on cycle day 3-5 day.1
    • Less data with Letrozole and IUI but suggest pregnancy rates same as clomiphene citrate with IUI in unexplained infertility.1
    • Letrozole without IUI has been shown to be superior to clomiphene in treating anovulation in patients with PCOS. 1
  • Timing:
    • Start urine LH testing on cycle day 10 to 12.1
    • After LH surge found in first morning specimen perform single IUI next day.1
      • Double IUI of uncertain value in studies and increases visits and cost.1

Prelabor Rupture of Membranes

  • < 23/24 weeks
    • Expectant management vs induction.
    • Latency antibiotics early as 20 weeks.
  • 23/24 - < 31 6/7 weeks
    • Expectant management.
    • Latency antibiotics.
    • Single course corticosteoids.
    • GBS prophylaxis if indicated.
    • Magnesium sulfate neuroprotection.
  • 32 - 33 6/7
    • Expectant management.
    • Latency antibiotics.
    • Single course corticosteoids.
    • GBS prophylaxis if indicated.
  • ≥ 34 weeks
    • GBS prophylaxis if indicated.
    • Deliver.1
  • 48 hours IV antibiotics:xxx
    • xxx
  • 5 days oral antibiotics:xxx
    • Low risk or unknown penicillin allergy:
      • Cephalexin.2
    • High risk penicillin allergy:
      • Clindamycin.2
      • Aziithromycin.2

      ACOG notes one study that after 3 days of IV antibiotics that target GBS it was no longer recoverable by culture and possibly using a regimen with IV vancomycin or oral clindamycin after 72 hours to just target GBS may not be required.2

  • With PPROM obtain GBS culture and start latency antibiotics that include GBS antibiotic prophylaxis (if GBS culture within 5 preceding weeks, previous GBS infected newborn or GBS bacteriuria in current pregnancy use to guide management).2
    • Continue antibiotics until delivery if labor progresses.2
    • Continue IV latency antibiotics for 48 hours if labor does not progress:2
      • GBS culture positive or not available with onset labor:2
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis.2
      • GBS culture negative in 5 week window with onset labor:2
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis "not necessary".2
      • GBS culture negative out of 5 week window with onset labor:2
        • GBS antibiotic prophylaxis.2
        • Repeat GBS culture.2
      • GBS culture negative out of 5 week window and no labor:2
        • Repeat GBS culture.2

5 week "screening accuracy window" based on early term screening studies and preterm data lacking2

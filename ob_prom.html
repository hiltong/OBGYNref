Prelabor Rupture of Membranes (PROM)

General:

  • Prelabor rupture of membranes (PROM): Before onset of labor.1
  • Preterm prelabor rupture of membranes (PPROM): Before labor and < 37 weeks1
  • Term prelabor rupture of membranes: Before labor and ≥ 37 weeks1
  • Preterm birth: 10% pregnancies US.1
  • Term prelabor rupture of membranes: 8% pregnancies US.1
  • Preterm prelabor rupture of membranes (< 37 weeks): 2-3% pregnancies US.1
  • Previable prelabor rupture of membranes: < 1% pregnancies US.1
  • Preterm delivery:1
    • 50% deliver within 1 week.1
    • Latency inversley correlated with GA of PPROM.1
  • Chorioamnionitis 15-35%.1
  • Postpartum endometritis 15-25%.1
  • Abruption 2-5%.1
  • Pulmonary hypoplasia (rarely fatal when PPROM after 23-24 weeks).1

PROM management by gestational age:

  • Delivery:1
    • GBS prophylaxis as indicated.1

Chorioamnionitis indication for broad spectrum antibiotics.1

  • Expectant mangement (less RDS):1
    • GBS screening & prophylaxis as indicated.1
    • Single-course corticosteroids if:1
      • Corticosteroids not previously given.1
      • Delivery planned ≥ 24 hours and ≤ 7 days.1
  • Delivery:1
    • GBS prophylaxis as indicated.1

    • Chorioamnionitis indication for delivery and broad spectrum antibiotics.1

  • Expectant mangement:1
    • GBS screening & prophylaxis as indicated.1
    • Latency antibiotics.1
    • Single-course corticosteroids.1
      • Can consider single rescue course corticosteroids.1
    • Magnesium sulfate neuroprotection if delivery anticipated < 32 0/7 weeks. 1

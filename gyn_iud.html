  • 5 years.1
  • Contains 52 mg of levonorgestrel.1

  • Insertion tube diameter 4.4 mm.1
  • Only IUD approved for menorrhagia.1
  • 5 years.3
  • Contains 19.5 mg of levonorgestrel.3
  • Releases ~ 17.5 mcg/day after 24 days, decreases to 9.8 mcg/day after 1 year and then to 7.4 mcg/day after 5 years. 3
  • Overall size 28 x 30 mm.3
  • Insertion tube diameter 3.8 mm.3
  • 3 years.4
  • Contains 13.5 mg of levonorgestrel.4
  • Releases ~ 14 mcg/day after 24 days, decreases to ~ 6 mcg/day after 1 year and to 5 mcg/day after 3 years.4
  • Overall size 28 x 30 mm.4
  • Insertion tube diameter 3.8 mm.4
  • 10 years.6
  • Overall size 32 x 36 mm.6
  • 3 mm diameter bulb at the tip of the vertical stem.6
  • Contraindicated if allergy to copper or Wilson’s disease.6
  • Emergency contraception:7
    • Most effective emergency contraception.7
    • Not affected by body weight unlike hormonal methods.7
    • Provides contraception if needed.7
  • Levonorgestrel-Releasing Intrauterine Devices:
    • Prevent fertilization by changing amount and viscosity of cervical mucus so it is impenetrable to sperm.7
  • Copper Intrauterine Device:7
    • Prevents fertilization by inhibiting sperm migration and viability.7

Preeclampsia with Severe Features

  • Systolic BP ≥ 160 or diastolic BP ≥ 110 on 2 occasions at least 4 hours apart unless BP treated before 4 hours.1
  • Platelet count < 100,000/microL.1
  • Elevated liver enzymes ≥ twice upper limit of normal or severe persistent right upper quadrant or epigastric pain unresponsive to medication and not accounted for by alternative diagnoses or both.1
  • Serum creatinine concentration > 1.1 mg/dL or a doubling of the serum creatinine in absence of other renal disease.1
  • Pulmonary edema.1
  • New-onset headache unresponsive to medication and not accounted for by other diagnoses.1
  • Visual disturbances.1

Older criteria included proteinuria > 5 g/24 hours and fetal growth restriction.1

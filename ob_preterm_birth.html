Preterm Birth

  • Leading cause of neonatal mortality in the United States.2
  • 10% all births.1
  • Spontaneous preterm birth:
    • Preterm labor (50% of preterm births).2
    • Preterm spontaneous rupture of membranes.2
    • Cervical insufficiency.2
  • Indicated preterm delivery:
    • Maternal indications.2
    • Fetal indications.2
  • Previous preterm birth:3
    • Increases 1.5 to 2 fold.3
    • Increases with the number of previous preterm births and decreasing gestational age at delivery. 3
    • Increases with previous preterm twin birth and decreasing gestational age at delivery (40% if previous twin birth < 30 weeks).3
    • Decreases if previous preterm birth followed by term delivery. 3
  • Short cervical length by transvaginal ultrasound:3
    • < 25 mm before 24 weeks.3
    • The shorter the cervical length the greater the risk.3
  • Others:3
    • Low maternal prepregnancy weight (BMI < 19.8)3
    • Short interpregnancy interval (lowest risk when 18-23 months). 3
    • Smoking.3
    • Substance abuse.3

Shoulder Dystocia

  • 0.2-3% all vaginal deliveries.2
  • 9-14% > 4,500 g birth weight.2
  • 20-50% > 4,500 g birth weight and diabetes.2

Note: Most shoulder dystocias occur in normal weight newborns and most macrosomic newborns don't have shoulder dystocia.2

  • Upper cervical C5 and C6 nerve roots.1
  • More frequent.1
  • "Waiter's tip" with adducted internally rotated shoulder and elbow extension and forearm pronantion.1
  • Lower cervical and upper thoracic nerve roots C8 and T1.1
  • Weakness of hand and medial forearm muscles.1
  • Macrosomia

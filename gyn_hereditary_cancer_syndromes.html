Hereditary Cancer Syndromes Related to Gyn Cancers

  1. Hereditary Breast and Ovarian Cancer Syndrome
    • Breast, ovarian, pancreatic, prostate, melanoma1
  2. Lynch Syndrome
    • Ovarian, endometrial, colon, gastric, biliary, pancreatic, renal pelvis, ureteral, glioblastoma1
  3. Li–Fraumeni Syndrome
    • Breast, colon, sarcomas, brain, adrenocortical1
  4. Cowden Syndrome
    • Breast, endometrial, colon, benign mucocutaneous lesions, thyroid, gastrointestinal hamartomas1
  5. Peutz-Jeghers Syndrome
    • Breast, ovarian, colon, cervical adenoma malignum, gastrointestinal hamartomas, pancreatic, gastric, small bowel1
  6. Hereditary Diffuse Gastric Cancer
    • Breast, gastric, colorectal1
  • Autosomal dominant with variable degress of penetrance1
  • Pesonal history:
    • Epithelial ovarian cancer, fallopian tube cancer, peritoneal cancer especially serous histology (10-15% heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome)1
    • Endometrial cancer (12% Lynch syndrome if DNA mismatch repair deficiency)1
    • Colon cancer (24% Lynch syndrome if DNA mismatch repair deficiency)1
    • Triple-negative breast cancer (30% heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome when < 60 years of age)1
  • Family history:
    • First degree relative with epithelial ovarian cancer1
  • Ancestry:
    • Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry (BRCA mutations)1

Fetal Growth Restriction

  • Fetal growth restriction: Estimated fetal weight -or-abdominal circumference < 10th percentile for gestational age.1
  • Small for gestational age: Birth weight < 10th percentile for gestational age.1

May fail to diagnose a larger fetus not achieving growth potential or misdiagnose a constitutionally small fetus achieving growth potential.1

  • Maternal conditions:1
    • Hypertensive disease (eg, chronic hypertension, gestational hypertension, or preeclampsia).1
    • Pregestational diabetes.1
    • Renal insufficiency.1
    • Autoimmune disease (eg, systemic lupus erythematosus).1
    • Cyanotic heart disease.1
    • Antiphospholipid antibody syndrome (One diagnostic criterion is previous pregnancy with a morphologically normal growth-restricted fetus requiring delivery < 34 weeks gestation).1
    • Substance abuse (eg, tobacco, alcohol, cocaine, or narcotics).1
  • Fetal conditions:1
    • Multiple gestation.1
    • Genetic disorders (eg, trisomy 13 (50%), trisomy 18 (50%), placental mosaicism identified by chorionic villus sampling.1
    • Congenital malformations (eg, congenital heart disease, gastroschisis).1
    • Teratogens (eg, cyclophosphamide, valproic acid, or warfarin).1
    • Infectious diseases (eg, malaria, cytomegalovirus, rubella, toxoplasmosis, or syphilis)1
      • Possible etiology in ~ 5–10% of cases of fetal growth restriction (Malaria accounts most common cause world wide).1
  • Placental abnormalities:1
    • Placental insufficiency most common cause fetal growth restriction.1
    • Placental disorders (eg, abruption, infarction, circumvallate, hemangioma, and chorioangioma).1
    • Placenta accreta and placenta previa not certain if causative1
  • Umbilical cord abnormalities:1
    • Velamentous or marginal cord insertion.1
    • Isolated single umbilical artery (~1% of all pregnancies) not certain if causative.1
  • Previous SGA birth (risk recurrence ~ 20%).1

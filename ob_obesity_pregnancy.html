Obesity/Pregnancy

BMI

Weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared (kg/m2).

  • Underweight
    • BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight
    • BMI 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight
    • BMI 25.0 - 29.9
  • Obesity class I
    • BMI 30.0 - 34.9
  • Obesity class II
    • BMI 35.0 - 39.9
  • Obesity class III
    • BMI ≥ 40
  • All adults ≥ 18 years of age and ≥ BMI 30 offered/referred for interventions for weight loss management (nonpregnant).1

Pregnancy

  • Underweight (BMI < 18.5):
    • Total weight gain: 28-40 lb1
    • 2nd & 3rd trimester weight gain/week*: 1 (1-1.3) lb/wk 1
  • Normal weight (BMI 18.5-24.9):
    • Total weight gain: 25-35 lb1
    • 2nd & 3rd trimester weight gain/week*: 1 (0.8-1)lb/wk 1
  • Overweight (BMI 25-29.9):
    • Total weight gain: 15-25 lb1
    • 2nd & 3rd trimester weight gain/week*: 0.6 (0.5-0.7) lb/wk 1
  • All obesity classes (BMI ≥ 30):
    • Total weight gain: 11-20 lb1
    • 2nd & 3rd trimester weight gain/week*: 0.5 (0.4-0.6)lb/wk 1

*Assuming 1.1-4.4 lb gain 1st trimester.1

  • 30.0-34.9 (Class I obesity)
    • Low-dose aspirin if another moderate risk factor* or a high risk factor**.2
    • Early glucose tolerance screening.1
    • Pneumatic compression device thromboprophylaxis before & after cesarean delivery.1
  • 35.0 - 39.9 (Class II obesity)
    • Low-dose aspirin if another moderate risk factor* or a high risk factor**.2
    • Early glucose tolerance screening.1
    • Pneumatic compression device thromboprophylaxis before & after cesarean delivery.1
    • Antenatal fetal surveillance 37 0/7 weeks.1
  • ≥ 40 (Class III obesity)
    • Low-dose aspirin if another moderate risk factor* or a high risk factor**.2
    • Early glucose tolerance screening.1
    • Pneumatic compression device thromboprophylaxis before & after cesarean delivery.1
    • Antenatal fetal surveillance 34 0/7 weeks.1
    • Weight based enoxaparin (Lovenox) 0.5 mg/kg every 12 hours starting 12 hours after cesarean delivery.1

*Moderate risk factors: Nulliparity, obesity, family history of preeclampsia (mother or sister), sociodemographic characteristics (African American race, low socioeconomic status), age 35 years or older, personal history factors (eg, low birthweight or small for gestational age, previous adverse pregnancy outcome, more than 10-year pregnancy interval).2

**High risk factors: History of preeclampsia, multifetal gestation, chronic hypertension, type 1 or 2 diabetes, renal disease, autoimmune disease (systemic lupus erythematosus, antiphospholipid syndrome).2

  2. Low-dose aspirin use during pregnancy. ACOG Committee Opinion No. 743. American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists. Obstet Gynecol 2018;132:e44–52.

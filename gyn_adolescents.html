Adolescents

  • Young-aged adolescents: 12–14 year olds
    • Concrete thinkers, poor or inconsistent abstract reasoning or problem-solving skills.1
  • Middle-aged adolescents:15–17 year-olds
    • Often assume they are invulnerable.1
  • Late-aged adolescents: 18-21 year-olds
    • Acquired problem-solving abilities and have relatively consistent abstract reasoning.1

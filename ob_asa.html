Low-Dose Aspirin

  • One or more high-risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Two or more moderate-risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Start 81 mg/day* at 12-28 weeks (optimally before 16 weeks) and continue until delivery.1

* Studies used 60-150 mg/day and not 81 mg/day but 81 mg/day only dose availbale in US reasonable.2

  • Previous unexplained stillbirth without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention fetal growth restriction without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention spontaneous preterm birth without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention of early pregnancy loss.1
  • Abruption.2
  • Postpartum hemorrhage.2
  • Fetal intracranial bleeding.2
  • High risk factors (Recommend low-dose ASA if ≥ 1 present as risk preeclampsia ≥ 8%)
    1. Previous preeclampsia.1,2
    2. Multifetal pregnancy.1,2
    3. Chronic hypertension.1,2
    4. Diabetes type 1 or 2.1,2
    5. Renal disease.1,2
    6. Autoimmune disease (SLE,APS).1,2
  • Moderate risk factors (Consider low-dose ASA if ≥ 2 present)
    1. ≥ 35 years of age.1,2
    2. Nulliparity.1,2
    3. BMI > 30.1,2
    4. Family histroy preeclampsia (mother or sister).1,2
    5. Sociodemographic factors (Africian american race, low socioeconomic status).1,2
    6. Autoimmune disease.1,2
    7. Personal history (e.g. SGA, previous adverse pregnancy outcome, > 10 year pregnancy interval).1,2
  • Low-risk factors (Low-dose ASA not indciated)
    • Previous uncomplicated term delivery.1,2

    • * Note all risk factors are historical.

  • Salicylate hypersensitivity.1
  • NSAID hypersensitivity.1
  • Nasal polyps.1
  • Aspirin induced asthmatic bronchospasm.1
  • History GI bleeding.1
  • Active PUD.1
  • Severe hepatic dysfunction.1
  • < 18 years of age and recovering from viral illness (especially influenza & chickenpox) secondary to risk Reye syndrome (<1%).1

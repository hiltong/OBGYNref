Low-Dose Aspirin

  • Recommended: ≥ 1 high-risk factors for preeclampsia as risk of preeclampsia at least 8%.3
    • Previous preeclampsia, especially if adverse outcome.3
    • Multifetal pregnancy.3
    • Chronic hypertension.3
    • Type 1 or 2 pregestaional diabetes.3
    • Renal disease.3
    • Autoimmune disease (SLE, APS).3
  • Recommended: ≥ 2 moderate-risk factors for preeclampsia.3
    • ≥ 35 years of age.3
    • Nulliparity.3
    • BMI > 30.3
    • Sociodemographic factors:
      • Africian american race.3
      • Lower income.3
    • Previous pregnancies:3
      • Small for gestational age.3
      • "Adverse pregnancy outcome."3
      • > 10 year pregnancy interval).3
    • In vitro fertilization.3
    • Mother or sister had preeclampsia.3
  • Consider: ≥ 1 moderate-risk factors for preeclampsia.3
    • Sociodemographic factors:
      • Africian american race.3
      • Lower income.3
  • Medically reasonable: 3
    • Offer to all patients if majority of patients in practice at moderate or high rsik for preeclampsia.3

    • * Note all risk factors are historical.

  • Start 81 mg/day at 12-28 weeks (optimally before 16 weeks) and continue until delivery.3
  • Previous unexplained stillbirth without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention fetal growth restriction without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention spontaneous preterm birth without risk factors for preeclampsia.1
  • Prevention of early pregnancy loss.1
  • Abruption.2
  • Postpartum hemorrhage.2
  • Fetal intracranial bleeding.2
  • Salicylate hypersensitivity.1
  • NSAID hypersensitivity.1
  • Nasal polyps.1
  • Aspirin induced asthmatic bronchospasm.1
  • History GI bleeding.1
  • Active PUD.1
  • Severe hepatic dysfunction.1
  • < 18 years of age and recovering from viral illness (especially influenza & chickenpox) secondary to risk Reye syndrome (<1%).1

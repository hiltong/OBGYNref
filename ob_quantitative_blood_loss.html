Quantitative Blood Loss in Obstetrics

  • Inaccurate estimation of blood loss is a leading cause of delayed response to postpartum hemorrhage.1
  • Quantification of blood loss shown more accurate than visual estimation.1
  • Benefit of quantification of blood loss by itself on clinical outcomes has not been demonstated.1
  • Quantification of blood loss is a component of obstetric hemorrhage bundles which have shown clinical beneift.1
  • Fluid volume = fluid collected before delivery of placenta (mostly amniotic fluid) is subtracted from total fluid collected after birth excluding irrigation (mostly blood after delvery).1
  • Blood soaked materials = weight of blood soaked materials minus dry weight (1 gram = 1 ml).1
  1. Quantitative blood loss in obstetric hemorrhage. ACOG Committee Opinion No. 794. American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists. Obstet Gynecol 2019;134:e150-6.

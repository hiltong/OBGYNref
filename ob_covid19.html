COVID-19 in Obstetrics

  • PAXLOVID
  • References
  • Includes:
    • Nirmatrelvir (SARS-CoV-2 main protease inhibitor).1
    • Ritonavir (HIV-1 protease inhibitor and CYP3A inhibitor).1
  • Indications:
    • Outpatient mild to moderate COVID-19 infection at high risk of clinical progression (12 years of age and older weighing at least 40 kg).1,2
      • Pregnancy as only risk factor1
        • Especially additional risk factors (e.g., body mass index >25, chronic kidney disease, diabetes mellitus, cardiovascular disease).1
      • Breast feeding with one or more risk factors.1
  • Treatment:
    • Initiate as soon as possible after diagnosis of COVID-19 and within 5 days of symptom onset.1
    • Orally with or without food.1
    • 300 mg of nirmatrelvir (two 150 mg tablets) with 100 mg of ritonavir (one 100 mg tablet), with all three tablets taken together twice daily for 5 days.1
  • Common OB drug interactions:
    • Contraindicated:
      • Methylergonovine.2
      • Oral midazolam (Versed).2
    • Caution as increases drug levels (monitor and may need to decrease dose):
      • Nifedipine.2
      • Fentanyl.2
      • IV midazolam (Versed).2
      • Betamethasone.2
    • Caution as decreases drug levels:
      • Ethinyl estradiol (nonhormonal contraception for 5 days of use and until one menses after stopping).2

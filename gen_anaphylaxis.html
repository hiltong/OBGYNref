Anaphylaxis Treatment In Adults

  • Cutaneous symptoms/signs most commom findings (10-20% no cutaneous finding): sudden onset of generalized urticaria, angioedema, flushing, pruritus.1
  • More emergent symptoms/signs: quickly worsening symptoms, respiratory distress, vomiting, abdominal pain, hypotension, dysrhythmia, chest pain, collapse.1
  • Stop probable cause if possible.1
  • Call for help.1
  • Immediate intubation if airway obstruction developing from angioedema.1
    • Complete obstruction can occur with delay and require cricothyrotomy.1
  • IM epinephrine (maximum dose 0.5 mg):1
    • No absolute contraindications with anaphylaxis, GIVE EARLY with mild symptoms before progresses.1
    • Decreases mast cell mediator release, treats airway obstruction and treats cardiovascular collapse.1
    • Patients 25-50 kg:1
      • 0.3 mL (0.3 mg) of 1 mg/mL solution IM mid-outer thigh.1
      • 0.3 mg by autoinjector1
    • Patients >50 kg:1
      • 0.5 mL (0.5 mg) of 1 mg/mL solution IM mid-outer thigh.1
      • 0.3 mg by autoinjector if drawing up causes delay.1
    • 1.5-inch needle to penetrate subcutaneous tissue if obese.1
    • Repeat every 5-15 minutes as needed.1
    • Most respond 1-3 doses IM, if not prepare IV epinephrine for infusion.1
  • Recumbent position and elevate lower extremities.1
    • If pregnant LLD.1
  • Oxygen nonrebreather mask at 15 liters/minute.1
  • Normal saline IV:1
    • 125 ml/hour if normotensive.1
    • 1-2 liter rapid bolus if hypotensive.1
  • Albuterol if bronchospasm resistant to IM epinephrine1
    • Nebulizer 2.5 to 5 mg in 3 mL saline, repeat as needed.1
    • Metered dose inhaler 2-3 puffs, repeat as needed.1
  • H1 antihistamine (for urticaria & pruritis)1
    • Cetirizine (Zyrtec) 10 mg IV.1
    • Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) 25-50 mg IV1
  • H2 antihistamine:1
    • Ranitidine (Zantac) 50 mg IV.1
  • Glucocorticoid: 1
    • Methylprednisolone (solu-medrol) 125 mg IV.1
  • Monitoring:1
    • Vital signs.1
    • Urine output.1
    • Pulse oximetry.1
  • Epinephrine continuous infusion by infusion pump if inadequate response IM epinephrine and IV saline.1
    • Titrate starting at 0.1 mcg/kg/minute.1
    • Non-weight-based dosing 2-10 mcg/minute if the weight uncertain.1
  • Second vasopressor (addition to epinephrine) if needed by infusion pump.1
  • Glucagon IV:1
    • If on beta-blocker may not respond to epinphrine (has inotropic and chronotropic effect not mediated by beta-receptors).1
    • Glucagon 1-5 mg IV over 5 minutes followed by 5-15 mcg/minute infusion.xxx

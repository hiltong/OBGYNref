Newborn Early Onset GBS Disease Prevention

  • Leading cause of newborn infection.1
  • Streptococcus agalactiae.1
  • Facultative gram-positive.1
  • Vaginal and rectal colonization can be intermittent, transient or persistent.1
  • 10-30% pregnant women have vaginal or rectal colonization.1
    • 50% Colonized women transmit GBS to newborn.1
      • 1-2% Newborns with GBS develop early-onset GBS disease without intrapartum antibiotic prophylaxis.1
  • Incidence newborn early onset GBS disease 0.23/1000 livebirths 2015 (80% drop from 1.8/1000 livebiths in 1990s).1
  1. Maternal genitourinary and gastrointestinal colonization.1
  2. < 37 weeks.1
  3. Very low birth weight (< 1500 grams | 3 lb 5 oz)1
  4. Prolonged ROM.1
  5. Amnionitis.1
  6. Young maternal age.1
  7. Black race.1
  • Previous GBS infected newborn.1
  • GBS bacteriuria any of colony count this preganncy.1
  • Prelabor intact menbrane c-section.1
  • ≥ 4 hours most effective.1
  • 2 hours shown to decrease vaginal colony counts and neonatal sepsis.1
  • Risk preterm birth.1
  • Preterm prelabor ROM.1
  • ROM ≥ 18 hours.1
  • Intrapartum fever (100.4°F/38°C)
    • Needs poly-microbial broad spectrum anitbiotics with GBS coverage as well (eg, ampicllin and gentamicin)1
  • GBS colonization in previous pregnancy.1

These are risk factors for newborn early onset GBS disease.1

Empiric GBS antibiotic prophylaxis is option.1

  • IV penicillin 1st line 1
    • IV ampicillin acceptable.1
  • IV cefazolin if low risk or uncertain risk penicillin allergy.1
  • IV clindamycin if high risk penicillin allergy and GBS susceptible to clindamycin.1
  • IV vancomycin only alternative if high risk penicillin allergy and GBS not susceptible to clindamycin.1
    • Use weight/renal fuction based dose 1
      • 20 mg/kg IV every 8 hours.1
      • Max 2 g per dose.1

    • Consider penicillin allergy testing if possible IgE mediated allergy or unknown severity of allergy.1

  1. Prevention of group B streptococcal early-onset disease in newborns. ACOG Committee Opinion No. 797. American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists. Obstet Gynecol 2020;135:e51–72

Emergent Obstetrical Hypertension Therapy

  • Systolic BP ≥ 160 or diastolic BP ≥ 110 persisting for 15 minutes.1
  • Severe systolic hypertension appears to be biggest risk for stroke.1
  • Treatment should occur within 30-60 minutes of diagnosis.1
  • 140-150/90-100 mm Hg1
  • If severe hypertension:1
    • Labetalol 20 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 10 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Labetalol 40 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 10 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Labetalol 80 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 10 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Hydralazine 10 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Emergency consultation, additional medications as ordered1 (i.e. end of order set)a.
  • No longer severe hypertension:1
    • BP every 10 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 15 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 30 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every hour x 4 hours.1

Avoid: Maternal asthma, heart disease, congestive heart failure.1

Risk: Neonatal bradycardia.1

  • If severe hypertension:1
    • Hydralazine 5 mg or 10 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Hydralazine 10 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Labetalol 20 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Repeat BP in 10 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Labetalol 40 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Emergency consultation, additional medications as ordered1 (i.e. end of order set)a.
  • No longer severe hypertension:1
    • BP every 10 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 15 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 30 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every hour x 4 hours.1

Risk: Maternal hypotension (systolic BP ≤ 90 mm Hg).1

  • If severe hypertension:1
    • Immediate-release oral nifedipine capsules 10 mg orally.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Immediate-release oral nifedipine capsules 20 mg orally.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Immediate-release oral nifedipine capsules 20 mg orally.1
    • Repeat BP in 20 minutes.1
  • If still severe hypertension (if below thresholds monitor as below):1
    • Labetalol 20 mg IV over 2 minutes.1
    • Emergency consultation, additional medications as ordered1 (i.e. end of order set)a.
  • No longer severe hypertension:1
    • BP every 10 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 15 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every 30 minutes x 1 hour.1
    • BP every hour x 4 hours.1

Risks: Increase maternal heart rate (but less overshoot hypotension).1

Immediate-release oral nifedipine (Procardia), not extended-release oral nifedipine (Procardia XL).1

Oral only, not sublingual or punctured.1

Monitor maternal vital signs when used with magnesium sulfate as both are calcium antagonists.1

  • Immediate-release oral nifedipine (first-line if no IV access).1
  • Labetalol 200 mg orally, may repeat in 30 minutes (if no IV access and immediate-release oral nifedipine not available).1
  • Nicardipine IV continous infusion.1
    • Calcium-channel blocker similar to nifedipine but more selective for cerebral and coronary vessels.2
  • Esmolol IV continous infusion.1
    • Very short-acting beta1-selective beta-blocker.3

. OB/GYN Nuggets

Gonorrhea

  • First line:
    • Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM x 1 + Azithromycin 1 gm po x 1.1
    • Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM x 1 + Doxycycline 100 mg po BID x 7 days.1
  • Second line:
    • Ceftriazone not available:1
      • Cefixime 400 mg po x 1 + Azithromycin 1 gm po x 1.1
      • Cefixime 400 mg po x 1 + Doxycycline 100 mg po BID x 7 days.1
    • Severe penicillin allergy:1
      • Gemifloxacin 320 mg po x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
      • Gentamicin 240 mg IM x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
      • Gentamicin 240 mg IM x 1 + Doxycycline 100 mg po BID x 7 days.1

Use first line if history of rash if no history of IgE mediated β-lactam allergy (anaphylaxis, Steevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis).1

  • First line:
    • Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM + Azithromycin 1 gm po.1
  • Second line:
    • Ceftriazone not available:1
      • Cefixime 400 mg po x 1 + Azithromycin 1 gm po x 1.1
    • Severe penicillin allergy:1
      • Gentamicin 240 mg IM x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
      • Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1 (needs test of cure 1 week).1

Use first line if history of rash if no history of IgE mediated β-lactam allergy (anaphylaxis, Steevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis).1

  • Test and presumptively treat sex partners in previous 60 days.1
  • Test and presumptively treat most recent sex partner if none in previous 60 days.1
  • Expedited partner therapy with cefixime 400 mg po x 1 + azithromycin 1 gm po x 1 if legal and no other option to treat.1
  • 7 days after patient and partner treated.1
  • Test of cure for treatment failure:
    • Not needed for uncomplicated gonrrhea of cervix/urethra/rectum treated with 1st or 2nd line regimen. Retest as abelow.1
    • 1 week if Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1 used in pregnancy.1
    • 14 days if pharyngeal gonorhhea treated with 2nd line regimen.1
  • Retest for reinfection:
    • Non-pregnant: 3 months.1
    • Pregnant: 3rd trimester.1
  • Positive culture ≥ 72 hours after treatment or positive NAAT ≥ 7 days after treatment and no sexual contact since treatment1
  • Suspect treatment failure with cephalosporin-resistant gonorrhea if:1
    • Persistant symptoms (3-5 days after treatment and no sexual contact since treatment).1
    • Postive test of cure (positive culture ≥ 72 hours after treatment or positive NAAT ≥ 7 days after treatment and no sexual contact since treatment).1
    • Positive test of cure culture with decreased cephalosporin susceptibility regardless of sexual contact since treatemnt.1
  • Before retreatment likley treatment failure with cephalosporin-resistant gonorrhea:1
    • Culture with antibiotic susceptibility testings and simultaneous NAAT. If gonorrhea isoltaed health department should send to CDC for confirmatory antibiotic susceptibility testing.1
  • Treatment likley treatment failure with cephalosporin-resistant gonorrhea:1
    • Consult health department/CDC.1
    • If previously treated with first line regimen:1
      • Gemifloxacin 320 mg po x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
      • Gentamicin 240 mg IM x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
    • If previously treated with Cefixime and azithromycin:1
      • Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM x 1 + Azithromycin 2 gm po x 1.1
    • After treatemnt.1
      • Test of cure 7-14 days after treatmentt with culture with antibiotic susceptibility testings and simultaneous NAAT.1
      • Test and presumptively treat sex partners previous 60 days.1

Pruritus in Pregnancy

  • ~ 23% of pregnancies.1
  • No pathologic cause most common.1
  • Pathologic causes:
    • Atopic eruption of pregnancy:1
      • Most common.1
      • Eczematous rash on the face, eyelids, neck, antecubital and popliteal fossae, trunk, and extremities. 1
    • Polymorphic eruption of pregnancy.1
      • Most common dermatosis of pregnancy:1
      • Pruritic urticarial papules and plaques on the abdomen and proximal thighs.1
    • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy:1
      • Generalized but predominantly affects the palms and the soles of the feet.1
      • Worse at night.1
      • Not associated with a rash.1
      • Elevated bile acids and liver function tests.1
    • Pemphigoid gestationis:1
      • Rare.1
      • Vesicles and bullae.1
  • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy:1
  • Other: (chronic renal failure, hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, liver disease, malabsorption,parasitosis or helminthosi, HIV, Hodgkin disease, leukemia, Non-Hodgkin lymphoma, polycythemia rubra vera, tumors (paraneoplastic), drugs [hydrochlorothiazide, opioids, etc.], multiple sclerosis,psychiatric disease [anxiety, depression, obsessive compulsive disorder].)1
  • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy
  1. Society for Maternal-Fetal Medicine. SMFM Consult Series #53: Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy. Am J Obstet Gynecol 2021.

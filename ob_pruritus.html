Pruritus in Pregnancy

  • ~ 23% of pregnancies.1
  • No pathologic cause most common.1
  • Pathologic causes:
    • With rash:
      • Related to pregnancy:
        • Early onset before 3rd trimester, involves limbs and trunk:2
          • Atopic Eruption of Pregnancy:1
        • Late onset 3rd trimester or postpartum, involves mostly abdomen.2
          • Polymorphic Eruption of Pregnancy:1
          • Pemphigoid Gestationis:1
      • Unrelated to pregnancy:
        • Psoriasis, etc.
    • Without rash:
      • Related to pregnancy:
        • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy:1
      • Unrelated to pregnancy:
        • Chronic renal failure, hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, liver disease, malabsorption,parasitosis or helminthosi, HIV, Hodgkin disease, leukemia, Non-Hodgkin lymphoma, polycythemia rubra vera, tumors (paraneoplastic), drugs (hydrochlorothiazide, opioids, etc.), multiple sclerosis,psychiatric disease (anxiety, depression, obsessive compulsive disorder).1
  • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy.1
  • Pemphigoid Gestationis.2
  • Spares umbilicus: Atopic Eruption of Pregnancy.2
  • Involves umbilicus: Pemphigoid Gestationis.2
  • Atopic Eruption of Pregnancy
  • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy
  • Pemphigoid Gestationis
  • Polymorphic Eruption of Pregnancy

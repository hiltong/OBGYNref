Hereditary Breast and Ovarian Cancer Syndrome

  • Germline pathogenic variants in autosomal dominant DNA repair genes with incomplete penetrance.1
  • BRCA1 and BRCA2 pathogenic variants most common.1
  • Other genes (eg. ATM, BRIP1, CDH1, CHEK2, NBN, NF1, PALB2, RAD51C, RAD51D) are a significant of cause heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndromes.1
    • Some (CDH1, CHEK2) increase breast cancer risk and not ovarian cancer risk.1
    • Some (BRIP1, RAD51C, RAD51D) increase ovarian cnacer risk and not breast cancer risk.1
  • 1/500 general population.1
  • 1/40 Ashkenazi Jewish population.1
  • Epithelial ovarian cancer, fallopian tube cancer, peritoneal cancer especially serous histology (10-15% chance heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome)1
  • Breast cancer (triple-negative breast cancer 30% chance heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome when < 60 years of age).1
  • Also pancreatic, prostate, and melanoma 1
  • Breast cancer
    • Women 65-74% lifetime risk.1
    • Men at risks with BRCA2 pathogenic variants.1
  • Ovarian cancer (ovarian, fallopian tube and primary peritonel cancer).
    • 39-46% lifetime risk BRCA1 pathogenic variants.1
    • 12-20% lifetime risk BRCA2 pathogenic variants.1
  • Pancreatic cancer.1
    • BRCA2 pathogenic variants.1
  • Prostate cancer.1
    • BRCA2 pathogenic variants.1
  • Melanoma cancer.1
    • BRCA2 pathogenic variants.1
  • 5-15% breast and ovarian cancers have BRCA1 and BRCA2 varinats.1
  • Personal history:
    • Epithelial ovarian cancer, fallopian tube cancer, peritoneal cancer especially serous histology (10-15% heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome)1
    • Triple-negative breast cancer (30% heriditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome when < 60 years of age)1
  • Family history:
    • First degree relative with epithelial ovarian cancer1
  • Ancestry:
    • Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry (BRCA mutations)1

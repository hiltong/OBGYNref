Orilissa (elagolix)

  • Oral GnRH receptor antagonist. 1
  • Moderate to severe endometriosis pain. 1
  • Safety/effectiveness in patients less than 18 years of age have not been established.1
  • Estrogen-containing contraceptives are expected to reduce the efficacy.1
  • Effect of progestin-only contraceptives on the efficacy is unknown.1
  • Advise use non-hormonal contraceptives during treatment and one week after discontinuing treatment.1
  • Dysmenorrhea and non-menstrual pelvic pain:1
    • Orilissa 150 mg QD.
    • Max duration 24 months.
  • Dysmenorrhea, non-menstrual pelvic pain + dyspareunia:1
    • Orilissa 200 mg BID.
    • Max duration 6 months.
  • Moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh Clas B):1
    • Orilissa 150 mg QD.
    • Max duration 6 months.
    • Mild hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh Clas A) does not need dose adjustment.
  • Pregnancy.1
  • Known osteoporosis.1
  • Severe hepatic impairment because of the risk of bone loss.1
  • Concomitant use of strong organic anion transporting polypeptide (OATP) 1B1 inhibitors (e.g., cyclosporine and gemfibrozil).1
  • Most common (>5%) hot flushes, night sweats, headache, nausea, insomnia, amenorrhea, anxiety, arthralgia, depression-related adverse reactions, and mood changes.1
  • Decrease in menstrual bleeding which may reduce ability to recognize occurrence of pregnancy.1
  • Bone loss.
    • Dose-dependent decrease in bone mineral density which is greater with increasing duration of use and may not be completely reversible after stopping treatment.1
    • Impact of decreases in bone mineral density on long-term bone health and future fracture risk is unknown.1
    • Consider assessment of bone mineral density in patients with a history of low-trauma fracture or other risk factors for osteoporosis. Do not use in women with known osteoporosis.1
  • Suicidal ideation and exacerbation of mood disorders.1
  • Elevation of serum alanine aminotransferase (ALT) .1
